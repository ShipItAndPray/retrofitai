How RetrofitAI Works

The Problem

Most homeowners waste money upgrading in the wrong order. They buy a heat pump for a drafty house. They install solar before insulating. They miss thousands in available rebates.

Professional energy audits cost $300-$500 and take weeks to schedule. Meanwhile, $8.8 billion in federal rebates (HOMES & HEAR programs) are rolling out state by state — and most people don't know if their state is even participating.

What Makes RetrofitAI Different

  • Building science sequencing — Envelope first (air seal, insulate), then HVAC, then controls. This is how professionals do it.
  • Interaction modeling — Insulating first means you need a smaller (cheaper) heat pump. We model these dependencies.
  • State-specific energy prices — Uses EIA residential energy price data by state, not national averages.
  • Real-time rebate status — Tracks which states have launched HOMES and HEAR rebate programs.
  • Climate zone matching — Your ZIP code maps to an IECC climate zone, which determines your baseline energy use.

Data Sources

  • NREL ResStock 2024.2 — 550,000 building prototypes with simulated energy use across all US climate zones (CC-BY-4.0)
  • EIA SEDS — State Energy Data System residential energy prices (2023-2024)
  • DOE SCEP — State and Community Energy Programs tracker for HOMES/HEAR rebate status
  • IECC/ASHRAE — Climate zone definitions from building energy codes
  • IEA — Heat pump and insulation interaction research

Limitations

  • Estimates are based on statistical building prototypes, not your specific house
  • Actual savings depend on air leakage rates, duct condition, occupant behavior, and local rates
  • Rebate programs change frequently — always verify with your state energy office before committing
  • Cost estimates are national medians with regional adjustment — get 3+ contractor quotes
  • This is NOT a substitute for a professional energy audit (BPI or RESNET certified)

Methodology

Baseline Energy Model

We use NREL's ResStock dataset to establish baseline energy consumption for each combination of IECC climate zone and home vintage. ResStock simulates energy use for 550,000 representative building models across the US housing stock using EnergyPlus.

Baselines are expressed in MMBtu/year for a 2,000 sqft reference home, then scaled by actual square footage with adjustments for insulation level, window type, and duct condition.

Scaling factors:

  • Insulation: Poor (+30%), Average (baseline), Good (-15%), Excellent (-30%)
  • Windows: Single pane (+20%), Double pane (baseline), Low-E (-10%), Triple (-20%)
  • Duct losses: Poor (+15% heating/cooling), Average (+8%), Good (baseline), No ducts (baseline)
  • Home type: Townhouse (-15% envelope), Duplex (-10%), Mobile (+20%)

Savings Calculations

Each upgrade has empirically-derived reduction factors for heating, cooling, water heating, and other end uses. These come from ResStock measure analysis and field studies.

Interaction effects: When multiple upgrades are recommended, we model diminishing returns. For example, if air sealing reduces heating by 15% and attic insulation by 20%, the combined effect is less than 35% because the second measure operates on the already-reduced load.

Fuel switching: Heat pumps and HPWHs replace combustion equipment with electric alternatives. Savings account for the COP difference (e.g., gas furnace at 95% efficiency vs. heat pump at COP 3.0).

Energy Prices

We use state-level residential energy prices from the EIA State Energy Data System (SEDS), converted to $/MMBtu for consistent comparison across fuel types:

  • Electricity: State average residential rate ($/kWh) × 293.07 kWh/MMBtu
  • Natural gas: State average residential rate ($/therm) × 10 therms/MMBtu
  • Propane: EIA weekly residential propane price by region
  • Fuel oil: EIA weekly residential heating oil price by region

Rebate Programs

The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 created two rebate programs totaling $8.8 billion:

  • HOMES (Home Owner Managing Energy Savings): Up to $8,000 for whole-home retrofits achieving 20-35%+ energy reduction. Performance-based.
  • HEAR (Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates): Point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for specific equipment (heat pumps, HPWHs, electrical panels, insulation). Income-qualified for full amounts.

State rollout timelines vary significantly. We track which states have launched each program based on DOE SCEP announcements and state energy office publications. Note: Federal 25C/25D tax credits expired December 31, 2025.
